ULTIMATE WORKFLOW PLAYLIST

EVERYONE IN FLOW

"Being completely involved in an activity for its own sake. The ego falls away. Time flies. Every action, movement, and thought follows inevitably from the previous one, like playing jazz. Your whole being is involved, and you're using your skills to the utmost." - positive psychologist Mihály Csíkszentmihályi.

Interruption is the enemy. More than anything else, at least in my opinion, an interruption at work can set you on a path to nowhere, far from the land of productivity and office glory. We're all here because we want to be and we're good at what we do. That being said, it doesn't mean we're not subject to the coffee jitters and the tedium of everyday that can make us lose focus on the task at hand. Anything from a meeting, to a bathroom break, to even just a quick question from your product manager can lead you astray, not to mention all the times you think about how upset you are that Sherlock "seasons" are only 3 episodes long and the fact that most of your work is done on a machine that can take you anywhere you want to go, virtually at least. So needless to say, we need all the help we can get to keep us in the zone, or as positive psychologists' put it, in flow.

Flow was coined by hungarian psychologist Mihály Csíkszentmihályi to describe the mental state in a which a person is so engaged in an activity that time flies and everything else fades into the background. According to him, "flow is completely focused motivation". Since we're all here because we want to be, doing what we each excel at, flow is completely within our reach. I'm a firm believer that every scene has a soundtrack, so I took to the streets of my music collection for help.

A steady stream of equally steady music can help keep everyone in the office on track. The playlist should be the perfect balance between upbeat and smooth. Dreamy and jazzy. The best way I can describe it—in obviously the most technical of terms—is the gentle waving motion of an arm outside a car window and the subtle bob and sway of someone's head as they feel the music. It keeps you alert and moving, but most importantly, it keeps you on track with steady rhythms. Music you can't help but groove to. No jarring riffs or spastic explosions of sound. No hooks too catchy to make you sing along, pulling you away from your work—just good music with smooth, hypnotic beats that hit the sweet spot between up- and down- tempo.

We can't eliminate all the evil, interrupting forces of the workday, but this music should help.

“Just keep it going”

Serves 1 or more, through headphones or speakers.
Works best with a cup of coffee and a fresh attitude.
