The Importance ofBeing Creative

All Articles

Next

Previous

The collective whole of advertising is a notoriously fast-paced industry. Each sector has its own trends and technologies to keep up with on a regular basis, in addition to the tumultuous work flow that often comes with the territory. Even when you’re fortunate enough to be doing something you legitimately enjoy for a living, the nature of advertising and our workaholic culture can quickly become frustrating and exhausting for even the most passionate folks.

Most of us have various interests outside of our daily work routine- it’s not uncommon for designers to be interested in fine art, music, video, crafts, and other creative fields, regardless of whether they’re designing a gig poster or a financial tracking application. If you’re drawing traditionally all day and meticulously inking sketches, checking out the latest UI trends and brushing up on CSS snippets could be cathartic, while the opposite might be true for someone working on computers for most of their waking hours.

Finding a balance between our various obligations, both societal and personal, can be a daunting undertaking. Many people have precious little time to accommodate relaxing, socializing, personal health, chores, errands, hobbies, and more, all in addition to their working life. Projects dreamed up in a short-lived flurry of inspiration fall by the wayside, collecting dust, along with the dentist appointment you know you need to make.

You can get in your few hours of nightly leisure time and a full night’s sleep, yet still start the day mentally and physically exhausted. This can especially be the case in advertising, where agencies often work like sweatshops to appease big name clients, and the return and reward factor on projects versus the time and effort put into them is not always fulfilling. While it can be difficult to put forth much effort into a side project or a craft after a day or week of applying yourself to work, whether it’s filing or construction, I think it’s a beneficial practice that can improve one’s mental health, outlook, breadth of knowledge, skills, and more. Becoming burnt out on your job is a reality that many of us face on a regular basis. Even with a wide variety of projects, there can be stifling repetition, let alone if you’re working on the same project over a long period of time.

So, if you like to knit, knit. If you like to paint, paint. If you like to write poetry, edit videos, make comics, code sites, illustrate, sculpt, cook, craft paper, sew costumes, work wood, shape metal, blow glass, or whatever, carve out a little time to pursue your other interests. You might be surprised by how rejuvenating it is and how accomplished you feel once you complete something. Get your mind off of work for awhile, refresh, and start anew.

We choose our careers (or most of us did, hopefully), but they alone don’t define us. We are a sum of all of our parts, even if it doesn’t always feel that way. When the nature of the beast has worn you thin and you feel as though your passion has all but left you, pour yourself into something else you enjoy, or even just find intriguing, and everything will be okay.

Miranda Petrosky

Experience Designer
