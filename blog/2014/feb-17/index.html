iWant to Believe

If you are an Apple lover like us, you have seen the rumors of Apple’s iWatch. You, like us, are hoping this is the product that will blow our minds the way the iPod did and iPhone did. Apple has maintained its great quality and design in the last couple of years but we have all been asking when the next great thing is coming? In October, Apple aficionados were expecting the iWatch to be released. Instead we got the iPad Air, whose only admirable feature was being thinner and lighter than its predecessors, something that should be a given for any tech product.

By far smartwatches are one of the most promising trends of 2014. Established players like Samsung and Sony tried to gain market share with their take on smart watches, while start-ups like Pebble and Basis struggled to make their brands household names. Currently, most smart watches seem to be aimed towards general market. However they are having difficulty getting past the early adopters or are having to limit their aim towards the fitness/wellness niche market. We have yet to see a smartwatch clearly breach the gap between the two. Furthermore, manufactures have failed to make a firm case on how smart watches are going to make our lives more efficient or better. Currently the smartwatch grants you the freedom from taking your phone out of your pocket. As of today smart watches are a great concept that has failed to materialize into a product with a value proposition that attracts the mainstream market.

If Apple does release the iWatch this year, expectations will be high, and Apple will probably deliver. Apple is a master in waiting until a market starts to develop, come out with their “Design by Apple in California” version, blow the first mover’s out of the water, and have their product become the standard. This has happened before with MP3 players and the iPod, and then again with touch screen phones and the iPhone. Because history is written by the victors, it will not matter that Apple wasn’t the first mover in the smartwatch market, they will set the standard for all following smart watches.

At least that is what we would have expected from Apple in the last decade. Something so groundbreaking that reading spec and features rumors would be useless because people cannot begin to fathom what it will do. But in the post Steve Job’s era, we can be more weary and the iWatch will probably look something like this. It will probably be better than any other smartwatch out there, but not necessarily a better value. It would be great if Apple skips the smartwatch fad and innovates with something more similar to the Google Glass or Intel’s Jarvis. Regardless, I am sure there will be heaps of people lining up outside Apple store’s worldwide when they do release whatever it is they are releasing.
