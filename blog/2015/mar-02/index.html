It's a Match

Tinder + Advertising

Previous

Tinder, the popular dating app among millennials, is making huge waves in the advertising industry. The app is visually-based, prompting users to “swipe right” if they like, or “swipe left” if they dislike a given user. Boasting approximately 30 million accounts with 14 million matches per day, Tinder provides advertisers a unique way to target consumers based on age, gender and location. Advertising on the platform started with a more guerilla marketing approach with “fake profiles” rather than paid advertising. Dominoes UK ran a Valentine’s Day promotion by creating their own profile and giving away free pizzas to users who “swiped right.”

Several agencies working for social causes utilized the platform to create awareness around certain issues. Advertising agency Eighty Twenty collaborated with the Immigration Council of Ireland to generate a conversation and raise awareness around sex trafficking in Ireland. Social Tees Animal Rescue partnered with advertising agency Bartle Bogle Hegarty to match animals up for adoption with new owners boasting over 1500 matches in only one hour.

Transitioning into native and paid advertising, several big brands have partnered with Tinder. USA was the first network to advertise on the platform running a campaign for their 2013 season premiere of Suits. Tinder profiles were created around one male and one female character from the show and if users matched, they were granted exclusive access to video and audio clips and sneak peeks. USA promoted the app on air and through social media during the premiere. FOX did something similar with their show, The Mindy Project in conjunction with a Tinder themed episode of the show. AMC also followed suit promoting their show Walking Dead on the app in New Zealand.

Tinder has also branched out of the TV network space and into professional sports and even big brand consumer products. The app sponsored “Swipe Right Night” at an Atlanta Hawks game in early January this year. This kiss-cam esque promotion was created to capitalize on the playful atmosphere at sporting events and drive attendance for the team (currently 24th in the league in attendance). There were suites for people who matched during the game where there were free roses and breath mints. There was engagement with Hawks social media accounts using “#swiperightATL” and the media coverage included a themed broadcast featuring a meshing of the Tinder and Hawks logos with sportscaster play-along with the event.

Gillette and their advertising agency MRY (of Publicis) utilized the app advertising tied to marketing research. Gillette paid Tinder to test the attractiveness of cleanly shaven vs. bearded males. 100,000 males with beards compared the number of matches in one week with their beards and one week without with the results presented on shavetest.com.

Stepping out of just purely paid advertising and into more marketing research and interactive space Tinder is flexing its advertising potential in many unexpected ways. There are serious implications of an app that can capitalize on a persons desire for acceptance and love.
