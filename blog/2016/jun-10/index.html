Snapchat’s New Discover Design Gets Cuddly With Advertisers

All Articles

Next

Previous

Snapchat has recently gone under the knife. The popular up-and-comer platform has blended its Discover and Live Stories with the purpose of attracting a larger audience to view the content publishers and brands create. This move edges Snapchat closer to Facebook’s native, in-feed advertising than ever before, which is as smart as it is necessary. Although brands like ESPN and Food Network spend significant time and funds to produce daily content for Discover, the engagement just wasn’t there in comparison to the user generated content of Live Stories.

The user interface on both the Stories and Discover screens now incorporates a gridded tile design, giving brands more of an opportunity to capture their audience’s attention. The option of utilizing images along with a headline is a departure from their previous design, opting for more information and delivering a blow for minimalism. They do, however, retain some of their subtlety with the new “subscribe” feature. Users simply hold down their finger at the end of a story to ensure future content from that publisher’s channel get added instantly to Recent Updates.

Make no mistake, the driving force behind these changes is fueled by advertisers’ desire to attract more traffic to their stories, not by what’s best for users. There’s no question that Snapchat derived much of its ad inventory revenue from Discover, but it simply wasn’t as effective as it could be. In order to keep attracting pocketbooks from big brands, their channel must achieve the desired exposure. And with ad blocking, programmatic / banner solutions coming under the microscope lately, moving towards native is smart.

As one of Snapchat’s 100 million plus daily users, I must applaud them. Snapchat did its best to represent advertisers while keeping us users on board. The social rebel continues to be smart and subdued in its hunt for more revenue while it slowly takes over more people’s online time. Users still have total control over what content they want to consume, giving them the power to decide if they want to invest time into the branded stories or not.

With our online world making a strong push towards quality versus quantity and repetition, Snapchat’s update is simply refreshing. Even if they nudged in favor of advertisers, the pressure continues to be on brands to make their content interesting enough for audiences to want to invest in. Will it yield higher completion rates for paid stories? I’m not sure yet. But as long as Snapchat continues to respect user privacy and show a clear understanding of how their audience interacts with their platform, I feel like they’ll get there and keep everyone happy while they do it.

My team and I are playing around with these new features already. I hope to see better content and better integrations of the platform moving forward. The stage has been set for us, so it’s up to us to deliver.

Erika Marquez

Interface Designer
