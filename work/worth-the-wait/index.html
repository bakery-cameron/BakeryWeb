WAITING A FEW MOMENTS CAN SAVE YOUR LIFE

Challenge

Union Pacific has made great efforts to advocate for safety near railroad crossings and tracks. However, most of train related accidents happen precisely when people attempt to skirt around the gate arms at a crossing to try to beat the train. Our job was to create a safety campaign that would make the consumer realize what they're putting at risk if they don't behave properly at these crossings.

Strategy

We targeted people that lived near railroad crossings, as well as people with an affinity for racing, extreme sports and other forms of thrill seeking. Because of how impatient our target is, we set out to craft a social media campaign that would communicate Union Pacific’s safety message as plainly and, more importantly, as quickly as possible. We picked the two social channels that best allowed us to connect with this audience.

YouTube

Youtube's TrueView was a great option for us. We used the platform's "5-seconds-to-skip" tool to communicate our message of patience. We showed viewers an active crossing after a train had just gone past, with the arms beginning to raise as soon as the video plays. By the time the 5 seconds to skip were up, the arms were completely raised and the viewer could skip to their video.

1

The time the user has to wait in order to skip the ad, would mimic the time he would have to wait at a crossing.

2

Once the user has waited a couple seconds, we hit them with the message and then encourage them to skip the ad.

3

After the 5 seconds are up, the user can skip to their intended video, directly conveying to them that it really wasn't so painful to wait for the payoff, even if it was likely just cats playing instruments.

Facebook

The campaign was extended to Facebook, where the storytelling was made more intimate. By not having the 5 second platform on this channel, our Facebook content was inversely designed to be more of a focal point for viewers. Our video series depicts scenarios in which a jogger, a young couple and a father and son have each stopped, waiting for a train to pass. When the arms lift, each story proceeds safely to tell how these individuals' spectacular future unfolds.

Production

Our goal was to publish content that didn't feel overproduced and could live naturally within Youtube's organic content. After filming at a real railroad crossing for a couple of very hot days in Beaumont, TX, we got just that.

Results

We loved having the opportunity to use a channel's platform to help tell our story. The stunt has helped deliver our message to millions of viewers nationwide and earned quite a bit of free coverage. Most importantly, it's helped spread Union’s Pacific safety message to those who need to learn about it most.
